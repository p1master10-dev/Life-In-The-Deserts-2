Life In The Deserts

DEFINATION

An Arid region characterised by extremely high or extremely low temperature and has scarce vegetation.

Types of Deserts

Hot Deserts

The desert climate or arid climate, is a dry climate sub-type in which there is a severe excess of evaporation over precipitation. The typically bald, rocky, or sandy surfaces in desert climates are dry and hold little moisture, quickly evaporating the already little rainfall they receive.

Example: Thar

Cold Deserts

These deserts are characterized by cold winters with snowfall and large rainfalls widely during the winter and occasionally over the summer. They occur in areas with extremely high rainfall periods such as the Antarctic and Greenland and also the Nearctic realm.

Example: Ladakh
